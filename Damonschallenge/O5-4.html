Object Class: Redacted

Special Containment Procedures:

O5-4 does not require any physical containment. The identity and actions of O5-4 are known only to a select group of Level 5 personnel. All communications and directives issued by O5-4 are to be logged and monitored for compliance with Foundation protocol.

Description:

O5-4 is the designation for the fourth member of the O5 Council, the apex governing body of the SCP Foundation. This individual has served in this capacity since the Council’s inception and is recognized for their extensive knowledge of containment protocols and strategic operations.

O5-4 serves as the head of the Foundation's Containment Research Division, responsible for developing and implementing advanced containment strategies for anomalous entities.

Responsibilities:

O5-4 oversees research into containment methods, coordinates with various departments to enhance security protocols, and evaluates potential containment breaches. They are also involved in high-level decision-making regarding the classification of new anomalies.

Abilities:

Strategic Thinker: O5-4 has an exceptional ability to analyze complex situations and develop effective strategies for containment and management of anomalies.

Leadership Skills: O5-4 excels in leading teams through high-pressure scenarios, ensuring that all personnel are aligned with the Foundation's goals.

Background:

O5-4’s history is largely classified. They have been pivotal in numerous containment operations and have significantly shaped the Foundation’s approach to handling Keter-class entities.

Addendum O5-4-A:

O5-4 played a crucial role in the containment of an anomaly, where their innovative strategies were instrumental in preventing a major breach.

Addendum O5-4-B:

Internal evaluations of O5-4's containment protocols have resulted in a proposal to standardize their methods across all Foundation facilities.

Incident Log O5-4-01:

Note:

Personnel are advised to treat communications from O5-4 with the utmost seriousness. The ramifications of O5-4’s directives can be significant, and adherence to their instructions is essential for the continued operation of the Foundation.
