Personnel Designation: O5-3

Object Class: Redacted

Special Containment Procedures:

O5-3 does not require any physical containment. The identity and actions of O5-3 are known only to a select group of Level 5 personnel. All communications and directives issued by O5-3 are to be logged and monitored for compliance with Foundation protocol.

Description:

O5-3 is the designation for the third member of the O5 Council, the apex governing body of the SCP Foundation. This individual has served in this capacity since the Council’s inception and is known for their expertise in psychological operations and personnel management.

O5-3 serves as the head of the Foundation's Psychological Health Division, overseeing the mental well-being of all personnel and ensuring that ethical standards are maintained in all research practices.

Responsibilities:

O5-3 is responsible for overseeing psychological evaluations, crisis management, and the implementation of mental health programs across the Foundation. They also provide guidance during high-stress situations and work to ensure personnel welfare is prioritized during operations involving anomalous entities.

Abilities:

Empathy and Insight: O5-3 possesses a profound ability to read emotional cues, enabling them to effectively manage personnel conflicts and crises.

Expert Negotiator: O5-3 is skilled in negotiation, often mediating disputes between departments and ensuring collaborative efforts are successful.

Background:

O5-3’s history is largely classified. They have played a crucial role in numerous incidents involving psychological anomalies and have significantly contributed to the Foundation's mental health initiatives.

Addendum O5-3-A:

O5-3 was instrumental in the aftermath of an incident involving SCP-073, helping staff cope with the psychological fallout and implementing support measures for affected personnel.

Addendum O5-3-B:

Recent assessments of O5-3's mental health initiatives have shown promising results, leading to discussions about expanding their programs across all Foundation facilities.

Incident Log O5-3-01:

During a Council meeting, O5-3 proposed the implementation of mandatory psychological evaluations for all personnel working with high-risk anomalies, which sparked a productive discussion on mental health standards within the Foundation.

Note:

Personnel are advised to treat communications from O5-3 with the utmost seriousness. The ramifications of O5-3’s directives can be significant, and adherence to their instructions is essential for the continued operation of the Foundation.
